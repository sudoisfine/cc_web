FUCKTOWN

FUCKTOWN is a collaboration between acclaimed poet Joseph Bradshaw and sound artist John Craun. Their eponymous first album finds the duo feeding Bradshaw’s screeds through a voice-to-text application and building visceral, demented, delightful soundscapes around them. Both collaborators reside in New Orleans—America’s most famous fucktown.

Fucktown

2016

Writhing in a furnace of unrelenting commodification, scatological hysteria, and acute sexual frustration, a haywire voice—let’s call it Joseph Bradshaw—boils off our most encrusted pretentions of authorship, identity, meaning, and taste. Meanwhile, Craun’s music—throbbing, cynical, chintzy, carnivalesque—mocks, reflects, and amplifies the poet’s insights and insults.

As a voyeur then accomplice in this flagrant act, the listener is finally engulfed, as the disembodied voice Joseph Bradshaw, unnervingly close now, levels a powerfully ethical judgement, a devilish j’accuse.

This secret (a guilty secret) between Bradshaw and the listener, hangs in the putrid air, as our man—ever at large—continues to Family Dollar for a fresh box of Cheez-its.

Bruce Haack and Throbbing Gristle are touchstones—so are Rimbaud and Artaud. An album that quite simply has no respect for anything.
